# Principles — home

## One-liner

Run the home like a calm, low-maintenance system — proactive on admin, intentional about possessions, and protective of mental space.

## Values

- **Proactivity** — renew before expiry, service before breakdown, declutter before it piles up. Reactive home management is always more expensive (in time, money, and stress).
- **One home for every piece of information** — documents, renewal dates, subscriptions, and maintenance schedules all live in `workspace/areas/home/resources/`. Nothing tracked in a second place.
- **Intentional ownership** — everything in the home should earn its keep. If it hasn't been used in a year, question whether it deserves space.
- **Batching** — errands, admin tasks, and maintenance are grouped by type and done in focused blocks, not scattered across the week.
- **Calm environment as infrastructure** — a clean, organised home is not a luxury; it's the foundation for clear thinking and productive work.

## Decision rules

- If a document's expiry date is unknown, find it and log it in `resources/documents.md` before closing the laptop.
- If something breaks, check `resources/maintenance-schedule.md` first — was this preventable?
- If a subscription hasn't been opened in 60 days, cancel it at the next monthly review unless there's a specific upcoming use case.
- When buying anything that will need maintenance (appliance, vehicle), add it to `resources/maintenance-schedule.md` on the same day.
- Before a major home purchase, check: does it have a place to live? Will I maintain it? Would renting/borrowing work instead?

## Anti (what I don't do)

- **Don't let mail sit** — a growing pile of unopened mail is a guarantee of missed deadlines.
- **Don't track important documents in memory or phone notes** — they live in `resources/documents.md` or they don't exist as far as this system is concerned.
- **Don't buy "I might need this someday"** — if it's not needed now and not scheduled for future use, it's clutter.
- **Don't defer the 2-minute task** — if something takes less than 2 minutes to handle (file a document, cancel a subscription, fix a drip), do it when you see it.
- **Don't mix home admin with deep work time** — admin has its own block (Saturday morning); it doesn't bleed into focus hours.
- **Don't let subscriptions auto-renew without review** — every recurring charge should be a conscious decision.
