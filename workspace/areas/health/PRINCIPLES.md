# Principles — health

## One-liner

Earn the right to work hard by recovering harder.

## Values

- **Consistency over intensity** — showing up 4 days/week for a year beats 7 days/week for 6 weeks then burnout.
- **Recovery is training** — sleep and rest are not optional extras; they are where adaptation happens.
- **Preventive over reactive** — annual check-ups, bloodwork, and sleep discipline are cheaper than treating illness.
- **Data-informed, not data-obsessed** — Whoop guides decisions; it does not dictate them.
- **Minimum effective dose** — do the smallest intervention that produces the desired result; don't add complexity until simpler things fail.

## Decision rules

- On a red-recovery day, default to rest or a walk — never override recovery data with willpower.
- If an injury nags for more than 5 days, see a physio rather than training through it.

- If sleep duration drops below 6 h for 3+ nights, cancel non-critical evening commitments that week.
- Supplements only when backed by blood results or clear physiological need — no marketing-driven additions.

## Anti (what I don't do)

- Do not train to failure every session — leave reps in the tank; injury risk is not worth marginal hypertrophy.
- Do not skip sleep to get more work done — cognitive output declines faster than the extra work hours add value.
- Do not obsess over the scale daily — weight fluctuates 1–2 kg from hydration; only the weekly trend matters.
- Do not start new diets or protocols without a 30-day data baseline first.
