# Principles — career

## One-liner

Build things that compound — skills, code, content, and relationships that grow while I sleep.

## Values

- **Leverage over hours** — seek work where output scales independently of time input; avoid trading hours for dollars indefinitely.
- **Depth over breadth** — genuine proficiency in a few domains beats surface knowledge of many.
- **Ship over perfect** — a working product in users' hands teaches more than a polished prototype in a notebook.
- **Reputation is a long game** — every public artefact is an asset that compounds; every shortcut is a liability.
- **Operator AND owner mindset** — capable of executing, but always asking "how do I build this so it doesn't need me?"

## Decision rules

- Evaluate any new commitment by its impact on deep-work hours — if it consistently costs > 3 h/week of protected time, decline or delegate.
- Take on consulting work only if the hourly rate is at or above target and it doesn't delay a product milestone. <!-- TODO: personalize — set consulting rate -->
- Before starting a new skill, check whether the existing skill roadmap already covers the need — don't add to the list, move things up or down.
- Before adding a new W3Mirror brand, evaluate whether an existing brand at deeper investment would produce more value (cf. `/go-deep`).
- Prioritise distribution over features on any product that already has a working core.
- Any non-reversible career decision (quitting, pivoting, major partnership) gets a 7-day written-reflection before commitment.

## Anti (what I don't do)

- Do not optimise for resume metrics (titles, logos) over real skill and leverage.
- Do not accept vague consulting briefs — clear scope, deliverable, and timeline or don't start.
- Do not mistake busyness for productivity — a full calendar that produces no deep work is a failure mode.
- Do not start a new project while an existing one is below first-revenue unless the old one is explicitly paused.
- Do not build in isolation — ship early, get feedback, iterate. No stealth-mode products.
- Do not neglect the network — relationships require maintenance; "I'll reach out when I need something" is too late.
- Do not let operational firefighting in W3Mirror brands crowd out strategic career development time.
