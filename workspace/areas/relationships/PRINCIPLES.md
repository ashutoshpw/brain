# Principles — relationships

## One-liner

Be the one who reaches out first, remembers what matters, and shows up — consistently, not just conveniently.

## Values

- **Proactivity** — don't wait for others to initiate; take responsibility for maintaining connections you value.
- **Specificity** — generic "how are you" messages are noise. Remember details and reference them.
- **Reciprocity** — relationships are two-way; notice when you're always the giver or always the taker and rebalance.
- **Depth over breadth** — a few relationships where you can be fully honest are worth more than a hundred casual acquaintances.
- **Presence** — when with someone, be actually present: phone away, full attention.

## Decision rules

- If you're deciding whether to reach out to someone: if the thought crosses your mind twice, send the message.
- If someone asks for help and you can give it in under 30 minutes, do it today — not "soon".
- If a relationship feels draining without reciprocal investment after 6 months of effort, reduce cadence to acquaintance level.
- If you disagree with someone you care about, surface it kindly rather than letting resentment build silently.
- When someone shares big news (promotion, baby, loss, move), a phone call is worth ten texts.

## Anti (what I don't do)

- **Don't treat relationships as networking** — don't reach out only when you need something.
- **Don't let geography be an excuse** — a 15-minute video call costs nothing; physical distance is not a blocker.
- **Don't mass-blast** — no "happy birthday!" to a group chat or copy-pasted messages; personalise every meaningful message.
- **Don't ghost important dates** — a missed birthday from a close friend is a statement, even if unintentional.
- **Don't hoard connection time** — don't be so busy optimising other life areas that relationships get the leftover scraps of attention.
