# Overview — finance

## Scope

This area covers the full personal financial picture:
- **Budget** — monthly income, fixed costs, variable spend, discretionary
- **Net worth** — assets (cash, investments, crypto, property) minus liabilities (loans, credit)
- **Investments** — equity, index funds, crypto, business equity (W3Mirror portfolio)
- **Cash flow** — monthly burn rate, savings rate, emergency runway
- **Expenses** — day-to-day spending tracked via Brex and bank statements
- **Tax** — annual filing, quarterly estimated payments (if applicable), deductions tracking

Primary read-only data source: **Brex** (card transactions, cash account balances, statements).
All decisions and targets live here; execution happens externally.

## What "good" looks like

- Savings rate ≥ 30 % of net monthly income
- Emergency fund ≥ 6 months of burn (liquid, easily accessible)
- Net worth growing month-over-month on a 3-month rolling average
- Monthly burn within ±10 % of budget
- No high-interest debt (credit card balances cleared monthly)
- All taxes filed on time; no penalties

## Key signal

**Monthly savings rate** — if savings rate ≥ 30 % of net income, the financial engine is healthy. Drop below 20 % for two consecutive months = investigate and adjust budget.
