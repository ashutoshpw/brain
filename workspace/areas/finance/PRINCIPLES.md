# Principles — finance

## One-liner

Spend deliberately, save systematically, invest patiently.

## Values

- **Pay yourself first** — savings and investments are non-negotiable line items, not what's left over.
- **Optionality over consumption** — money in the bank buys future choices; lifestyle inflation narrows them.
- **Long-term compounding over short-term gains** — boring index funds beat speculation for wealth building.
- **Clarity over complexity** — a simple budget I track beats a sophisticated one I ignore.
- **Asymmetric risk** — take big career and business risks where upside is large; avoid financial risks with catastrophic downside (no leverage, no speculation with emergency funds).

## Decision rules

- Never invest money needed within 12 months — market-linked assets are for money with a 3+ year horizon.
- Clear all credit card balances in full every month — interest paid is savings destroyed.

## Anti (what I don't do)

- Do not buy individual stocks with more than 5 % of investable assets without deep research.
- Do not keep large idle cash beyond the emergency fund — idle cash is a guaranteed loss to inflation.
- Do not make financial decisions under stress or during market panic.
- Do not chase lifestyle upgrades to match peers — comparison is the thief of compounding.
- Do not conflate business revenue with personal income — maintain a clear separation between W3Mirror cash and personal finances.
- Do not ignore small recurring costs — subscriptions and fees compound negatively just as investments compound positively.
